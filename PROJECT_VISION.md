# HFC Geometry Kernel Project Vision

## Version Roadmap
- Version 0.1: foundational geometry primitives, transformations, precision control, and basic tests.
- Version 0.2: mesh integration, validation workflows, and improved geometric utilities.
- Version 0.3: reverse engineering support for feature extraction and reconstruction strategies.
- Version 0.4: CAD-oriented modeling, export compatibility, and richer semantic representation.
- Version 1.0: a stable, documented kernel for professional engineering and research use.

## Long-Term Objectives
- Build a trusted geometry engine for industrial and research applications.
- Become the mathematical backbone for reverse engineering, design automation, and digital twin workflows.
- Support both manual engineering workflows and AI-assisted interpretation without sacrificing rigor.
- Provide a platform that can evolve into a broader geometry and modeling ecosystem.

## Reverse Engineering Roadmap
- Import and analyze polygonal meshes with robust preprocessing.
- Detect geometric primitives and candidate features from raw scan data.
- Reconstruct structured CAD-like surfaces and boundaries from imperfect inputs.
- Support inspection, comparison, and reconstruction pipelines with explicit validation.

## CAD Roadmap
- Expand from primitives toward parametric and feature-based representations.
- Introduce richer boundary and topology concepts for solid modeling workflows.
- Improve interoperability with common CAD exchange formats and downstream modeling tools.
- Strengthen support for design intent, constraints, and editable geometry.

## AI Roadmap
- Use AI for guidance, suggestion, and interpretation of ambiguous geometry.
- Keep AI outputs constrained by deterministic kernel validation.
- Enable human-in-the-loop workflows where AI proposes options and the kernel verifies them.
- Develop assistive features that improve speed without weakening mathematical confidence.
